How it works

Dictation without the friction.

No window to switch to, nothing to copy and paste. It works wherever your cursor is — email, chat, code, notes.

STEP 01

Hold the key

Press and hold Left ⌥ from anywhere. Or tap Right ⌥ once for hands-free.

STEP 02

Say it out loud

Speak naturally. A red dot in your menu bar shows freeflo is listening — right on your Mac, never the cloud.

STEP 03

Let go

Release the key and your words appear at the cursor, instantly, in whatever app you're using.

Why freeflo

Small app. Serious respect for your privacy.

Built on whisper.cpp, freeflo runs the speech model directly on your Mac — so it's fast, works on a plane, and sends nothing anywhere.

🔒

Truly offline

No internet required. Your voice is transcribed on-device and never leaves your machine.

🌐

Speaks your language

English, Hindi, Hinglish, Spanish, French, German, Chinese, Japanese, Arabic, Portuguese — or auto-detect.

✍️

Types anywhere

Works in any app and restores your clipboard afterward, so nothing you'd copied gets lost.

⌥

Two ways to talk

Push-to-talk for a quick sentence, or toggle mode for hands-free, long-form dictation.

🕑

Searchable history

Every transcription is saved locally so you can find and reuse it later. Turn it off anytime.

🎙

Lives in the menu bar

No dock clutter, no window in the way. Just a quiet icon that's there when you need it.

Download & install

Up and running in about two minutes.

macOS 12 Monterey or later Apple Silicon or Intel A microphone ~1.5 GB free space
Download freeflo .zip · 644 MB
1

Download and unzip

Click Download freeflo above. When it finishes, double-click freeflo.zip in your Downloads to unzip it — you'll get freeflo.

2

Move it to Applications

Drag freeflo into your Applications folder. That's where Mac apps live.

3

Open it the first time

Because freeflo is from an independent developer (not the App Store), macOS checks with you first. Double-click it — if you see “Apple could not verify…”, click Done.

Then open System Settings → Privacy & Security, scroll down, and click Open Anyway next to freeflo. Confirm with Open. You only do this once.

4

Allow the microphone & keyboard

freeflo needs two permissions: the Microphone (to hear you) and Accessibility (to type into other apps and use the hotkey). macOS will prompt you — say yes.

If the hotkey doesn't work, go to System Settings → Privacy & Security → Accessibility, switch freeflo on, and relaunch it.

5

Look up — you're ready

Find the 🎙 icon in your menu bar. Put your cursor anywhere, hold Left Option (⌥), say a sentence, and let go. Welcome to hands-free typing.

📦
Why 644 MB? The download bundles the complete speech-recognition models right inside the app, so freeflo works fully offline the moment you open it — no extra setup, no downloads, no sign-in.

Still blocked after step 3? Open Terminal and run xattr -dr com.apple.quarantine /Applications/freeflo.app, then try again. Prefer to build from source instead? See the README on GitHub.
