Vision · why we build

Talk to your machine the way you talk to a person.

Speaking is the highest-bandwidth interface humans have. freeflo brings it to your devices — so you can say what you want and get it done faster. Voice-to-text is chapter one, not the whole book.

Every interaction with your system should be better than it was before — and it should stay yours.
The north star

The problem

We still translate what we mean into what the machine accepts.

Menus, windows, shortcuts, copy-paste, context switches. Talking to a person is instant and natural; talking to a computer is the opposite. That translation layer is where time and flow are lost. We want to erase it — no window to open, no app to switch to, no cloud round-trip. You say it; your machine does it.

The arc

One interface, expanding across domains — and eventually across devices.

freeflo starts on the Mac with dictation because it's the most universal, immediate win. But each new domain is added the same way: by letting you speak instead of click.

  1. Todayshipping

    Say it → typed

    Hold a key, speak, and your words appear at the cursor in any app — transcribed entirely on your own machine.

  2. Next

    Manage your time by speaking

    Capture, schedule, and reshape your day out loud instead of navigating a calendar.

  3. Next

    Manage your work by speaking

    Tasks, notes, and context handled by voice — the busywork of organizing, spoken away.

  4. Then

    Drive your machine by speaking

    Control the laptop itself, not just fill text fields. Make working on your system easier, end to end.

  5. Then

    Work better with LLMs

    Talk to models with the same privacy guarantees as everything else freeflo does.

  6. Horizon

    Beyond the Mac

    The same natural interface across every device you own.

What we believe

Six principles that constrain the roadmap, the architecture, and the business.

01

On-device first

Everything that can run locally, runs locally. That buys two things at once: low latency — no network round-trip — and data ownership.

02

You own your data

What you say and dictate is yours. Transcribed text and audio never leave the device — enforced by architecture, not a policy promise. Telemetry is metadata only, consented, and switchable off.

03

Free — or a small one-time price

No subscriptions, no word limits, no rent on your own voice. On-device compute means no server bill to pass on to you. If we ever charge, it's a minimal one-time price for a capability — never a meter.

04

Built for people who live in their systems

Our users are professionals — many technical. We build a serious tool for people who work all day on their machines. The bar is "indispensable," not "fun to try once."

05

Evolves with its users

Not a one-feature company that ships a gimmick and vanishes. The in-app feature request channel exists so we hear what users need and evolve the product with them.

06

Privacy is the design language, too

Neutral, precise, futuristic, permanent-feeling. How freeflo looks signals what it is: a serious, private, on-device tool you can trust — not a disposable app.

Business & design

Why the economics and the look both point the same way.

The economics make the promise sustainable

On-device compute means near-zero marginal cost per user — which is what makes "free / minimal one-time price" real, not a loss-leader promise.

  • →
    No backend today. On-device plus managed free services — updates via GitHub Releases, consented analytics on managed tiers.
  • →
    Add a backend only on a concrete trigger — owning our data end-to-end, server-side logic, or hosted LLM features. Never by default.
  • →
    The moat is trust + depth across domains, compounding as we listen and expand.

The look says "serious and permanent"

The brand is deliberately neutral and monochrome: a living waveform chip, a chromed uppercase wordmark, hairline structure, HUD/terminal precision cues (lockup A+1).

We chose it because freeflo is a trust product — it handles your voice and your data on your own machine. The visual language has to say "serious tool that will endure," not "fun feature that will vanish."

See the brand system, onboarding, and the app window.

The line we never cross

Your words never leave your machine.

Transcribed text and audio never leave the device. Telemetry is metadata + profile only, and always consented. If a future feature can't honor this, it either runs on-device — or it doesn't ship without explicit, informed, opt-in consent.
